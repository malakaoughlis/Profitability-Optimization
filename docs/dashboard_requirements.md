# Dashboard Requirements

## Project

**Profitability Optimization - Business Intelligence Case Study**

## Main Business Question

**How can Nova Retail improve profitability without simply increasing sales?**

## Dashboard Objective

The objective of this dashboard is to help management understand what drives profitability across sales, products, customers, markets, discounts, and shipping.

The dashboard should not only show numbers. It should help answer business questions and support decision-making.

---

# Dashboard Structure

The Power BI dashboard will be divided into four pages:

1. Executive Overview
2. Profitability Analysis
3. Discount Impact
4. Market and Operations

Each page must answer a specific business question and guide the user toward clear insights.

---

# Page 1 - Executive Overview

## Objective

Give management a quick overview of overall business performance.

This page should help the executive team understand whether Nova Retail is profitable and whether sales growth is aligned with profit growth.

## Key Business Questions

- Is the company profitable overall?
- Are sales and profit growing together?
- What is the overall profit margin?
- How many order lines generate losses?
- Which markets and categories contribute the most to profit?

## KPIs

- Total Sales
- Total Profit
- Profit Margin
- Total Orders
- Total Customers
- Loss-Making Row Share

## Recommended Visuals

- KPI cards for total sales, total profit, profit margin, orders, customers, and loss-making row share
- Sales by year
- Profit by year
- Profit margin by year
- Profit by market
- Profit by category

## Suggested Filters

- Year
- Market
- Region
- Category
- Segment

## Main Message

Nova Retail is profitable overall, but some parts of the business reduce overall performance.

This page should quickly show that revenue alone is not enough to evaluate business success.

---

# Page 2 - Profitability Analysis

## Objective

Identify what reduces profitability.

This page should help management understand which products, categories, or sub-categories create profit and which ones reduce business performance.

## Key Business Questions

- Which categories are most profitable?
- Which sub-categories lose money?
- Which products generate losses?
- Do high sales always mean high profit?
- Which products have high revenue but weak profitability?

## KPIs

- Total Profit
- Profit Margin
- Loss-Making Rows
- Sales vs Profit
- Number of Loss-Making Products

## Recommended Visuals

- Profit by category
- Profit margin by category
- Top sub-categories by profit
- Bottom sub-categories by profit
- High-sales / low-margin products table
- Loss-making products table
- Sales vs profit scatter plot by product

## Suggested Filters

- Year
- Category
- Sub-Category
- Market
- Segment

## Main Message

Revenue alone is not enough. Some products generate strong sales but reduce profit.

This page should help identify which products require pricing, discount, or strategy review.

---

# Page 3 - Discount Impact

## Objective

Understand how discounts affect profitability.

This page should help management evaluate whether the current discount strategy supports profitable growth or destroys margin.

## Key Business Questions

- Do discounts improve profit?
- At what discount level does profit become negative?
- Which discount bands are dangerous?
- Are high discounts linked to loss-making transactions?
- Which categories are most affected by discounts?

## KPIs

- Sales by Discount Band
- Profit by Discount Band
- Profit Margin by Discount Band
- Loss Rate by Discount Band
- Average Discount

## Recommended Visuals

- Sales by discount band
- Profit by discount band
- Profit margin by discount band
- Loss rate by discount band
- Profit margin by discount level
- Discount impact by category

## Suggested Filters

- Year
- Category
- Sub-Category
- Market
- Segment
- Discount Band

## Main Message

High discounts can increase sales but destroy profitability.

This page should clearly show whether discount policies need to be reviewed, especially for low-margin products or underperforming categories.

---

Analyze profitability by geography and shipping performance.

This page should help management understand where the business performs well, where it underperforms, and whether shipping costs affect profitability.

- Which markets generate the most profit?
- Which regions underperform?
- Which countries generate losses?
- Do shipping costs affect profitability?
- Which shipping modes are the most cost-efficient?
- Does order priority affect profit?

## KPIs

- Profit by Market
- Profit Margin by Region
- Top Countries by Profit
- Bottom Countries by Profit
- Shipping Cost Ratio
- Profit by Ship Mode

## Recommended Visuals

- Sales by market
- Profit by market
- Profit margin by region
- Top countries by profit
- Bottom countries by profit
- Shipping cost ratio by ship mode
- Profit by ship mode
- Profit by order priority

## Suggested Filters

- Year
- Market
- Region
- Country
- Ship Mode

# Design Principles

## What the dashboard should do

- Start with business questions, not charts.
- Keep each page focused on one main topic.
- Show KPIs at the top of each page.
- Use simple and readable visuals.
- Make profit and margin more visible than sales alone.
- Help users compare performance across products, customers, markets, and discounts.
- End with insights that support business decisions.

## What the dashboard should avoid

- Too many visuals on one page.
- Charts that do not answer a business question.
- Overloaded colors or unnecessary design effects.
- Focusing only on sales while ignoring profit.
- Using visuals without explaining their business meaning.

---

# Expected Business Insights

At the end of the dashboard, management should be able to identify:

- which products drive profit;
- which products reduce profitability;
- which customer segments are valuable;
- which markets deserve more attention;
- whether discounts are helping or hurting the business;
- whether shipping costs should be optimized;
- where strategic action is needed to improve profitability.

---

# Expected Business Impact

| Recommendation Area | Expected Impact |
|---|---|
| Reduce excessive discounts on low-margin products | Improve overall profit margin |
| Focus on high-profit products and categories | Increase return on sales |
| Review loss-making products and sub-categories | Reduce financial losses |
| Prioritize profitable markets and regions | Support profitable growth |
| Optimize shipping methods and order priorities | Lower operational costs |
| Track loss-making transactions | Improve profitability monitoring |

---

# Dashboard Success Criteria

The dashboard will be considered successful if it allows a business user to quickly answer:

1. Is Nova Retail profitable?
2. Where does profit come from?
3. Which products or categories reduce profitability?
4. Are discounts improving or damaging business performance?
5. Which markets and regions deserve more attention?
6. Are shipping costs affecting margins?
7. What business actions should management consider next?
